KEY FINDINGS
-------------
1. Truncation is the dominant failure mode. Both presses frequently cut off
   mid-sentence, suggesting max_new_tokens is set too low. This is a generation
   parameter issue, not a compression issue.

2. Factual accuracy is generally preserved. When answers are not truncated,
   both presses tend to retrieve the correct information from the compressed
   context. Errors are more often due to incompleteness than hallucination.

3. KVzipPress outperforms ObservedAttentionPress overall (3.6 vs 3.2),
   particularly on completeness. The gap is most visible in the Burpee and
   Teachers Union cases.

4. ObservedAttentionPress produced a garbled output on one question in the
   Teachers Union case ("-1|jurisdiction:" prefix), suggesting occasional
   instability with this press method.

5. The legal malpractice case (Don v. Soo Hoo) was the hardest for both
   presses, with both averaging 2.6. This case may require more nuanced
   reasoning across multiple parts of the document.


RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS
------------------------
- Increase max_new_tokens to reduce truncation and re-evaluate.
- Test additional compression ratios (e.g. 0.3, 0.7) to measure sensitivity.
- Run the fifth case (super-ct-jud_1_html_0258-01) and include in final results.
- Consider using an automated LLM-as-judge scoring pipeline for scalability.
