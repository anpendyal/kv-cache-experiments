KEY FINDINGS
-------------
1. KVzipPress consistently outperforms ObservedAttentionPress (4.7 vs 4.2).
   The gap is most pronounced on the longest and most complex document
   (super-ct-jud_1_html_0258-01), where OBS averaged 3.6 vs ZIP's 4.6.

2. ObservedAttentionPress has a recurring "-1|jurisdiction:" artifact that
   appears on the same question (CBA second basis) across multiple runs,
   suggesting a systematic issue with how OBS handles that particular
   compressed context, not a random fluke.

3. ObservedAttentionPress produces a complete failure ("-1") on the Grand
   Jury outcome question consistently across runs. This is the longest
   document and the question requires finding a specific brief note at the
   end of a very long text. OBS appears unable to locate it after compression.

4. ObservedAttentionPress hallucinates case citations reliably. It fabricated
   "Hoff v. Zimmerman" and "Symmes v. Davis" and "Bradley v. Bradley" when
   the correct citations were "Hoffman v. Savage", "Symmes v. Drew", and
   "Baker v. Willard". KVzipPress cited correctly in all cases. This is a
   meaningful difference for legal document evaluation.

5. Both presses consistently mischaracterize the concurring judge's suggestion
   in Don v. Soo Hoo across all runs. This appears to be a genuine compression
   artifact — both presses lose the nuance that Soo Hoo should have paid Don
   what she requested (more than just the $1,000 fee), not just refunded the fee.

6. KVzipPress scores are highly stable across runs (4.7 in both runs 2 and 3),
   suggesting reliable compression behavior. OBS scores show more variability.

7. Neither press is well-suited for questions requiring retrieval of brief
   specific facts buried at the end of very long documents, but ZIP handles
   this significantly better than OBS.


RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS
------------------------
- Investigate the "-1|jurisdiction:" OBS artifact — likely a tokenization or
  attention pattern issue specific to the Granite model architecture.
- Test on a supported model (Qwen2, LLaMA) since Granite is flagged as
  untested by kvpress, which may contribute to OBS instability.
- Test higher and lower compression ratios (0.3, 0.7) to measure sensitivity.
- Add a ground-truth baseline run (no compression) to measure absolute
  degradation rather than relative comparison between the two presses.
- Expand to more cases for statistical robustness given the small sample size.
